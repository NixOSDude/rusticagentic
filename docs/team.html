Founder & Lead Developer
Scott Baker
Founder & Solo Developer — Systems, AI & Cryptography

Scott is a systems engineer whose career spans web development, enterprise IT, information security, data engineering, and sovereign AI infrastructure. His relationship with computing started in the 1980s on a Mac SE/30 with HyperCard at Haverford Preparatory School — that instinct for building interactive systems has driven every project since.

In the early 2000s he ran his own architectural design firm, working alongside attorneys daily. He watched them depend on LexisNexis to search legal records — expensive, cloud-dependent, and outside the firm's control. When he later began building a Rust-based RAG system, that experience pointed him directly at legal AI as the right market.

Since late 2022, long-term disability has ended his ability to work onsite. He has used that time for something most engineers never get: uninterrupted, full-time technical immersion. No commute, no meetings, no context-switching — just deep, sustained study of the hardest problems in systems programming, AI infrastructure, and applied cryptography.

He holds an AWS Certified Solutions Architect and a Databricks Certified Developer for Apache Spark (Scala track). He completed a six-month structured Python RAG AI course, then deliberately rebuilt the entire pipeline from scratch in Rust — no frameworks, no external AI APIs, every layer owned. He has developed deep expertise in prompt engineering for legal domain accuracy, and has studied Kubernetes, post-quantum cryptography (NIST FIPS 203/204), CUDA GPU computing, and distributed systems architecture in parallel with production code.

His web roots in hosting began in 2012 at Endurance International Group and GoDaddy — Linux servers, VPS environments, and high-traffic web hosting at scale. That foundation informs everything about how RusticAgentic deploys. He is also the inventor of skr8tr — a sovereign, masterless UDP distributed mesh framework built in C23 with post-quantum authenticated command propagation and no single point of failure.
