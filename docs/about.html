The Origin Story

Scott has been on long-term disability since late 2022, which ended his ability to work onsite. Rather than stepping back from engineering, he stepped all the way in. With nothing but time and the discipline to use it, he has spent those years in a level of technical immersion that a conventional career simply does not permit.

He began with Rust — working through the language until systems-level memory management, ownership semantics, and async concurrency became second nature. He then went deeper: C23 systems programming, CUDA GPU kernels, DuckDB columnar analytics, and multi-node Apache Spark in functional Scala. He earned his AWS Certified Solutions Architect and Databricks Certified Developer — both backed by production code, not just exam prep.

He spent six months in a structured, in-depth Python RAG AI course — mastering retrieval-augmented generation from vector indexing to LLM prompting at the theory level. He then made a deliberate choice: discard the Python frameworks entirely and rebuild the full RAG pipeline from scratch in Rust, owning every layer himself. He studied Kubernetes orchestration and post-quantum cryptography (ML-KEM, ML-DSA — NIST FIPS 203/204) in parallel. He also developed serious depth in prompt engineering — system prompt construction, retrieval-aware context assembly, and domain-specific instruction design for legal AI accuracy.

Those years also produced a second invention: skr8tr — a sovereign, masterless UDP distributed mesh framework for web-scale workloads. No certificate authorities, no central broker, no single point of failure. Every packet is post-quantum authenticated with ML-DSA-65. Built in C23 and born from the same web infrastructure roots that started Scott's career at Endurance International and GoDaddy.

The result is RusticAgentic — a platform that could only be built by someone with the time and discipline to own every layer: the cryptography, the embedding pipeline, the vector index, the document extraction, the GPU inference server, and the encrypted vault. No external AI APIs. No shared infrastructure. No data egress. Just a system that does exactly what it claims, provably, on hardware the client controls.
